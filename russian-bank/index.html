How to play (Russian Bank / Zank-Patience / Crapette)

  • Each player has their own deck split into a Reserve (top card face up), a Hand (face down), a Waste pile, and 4 Houses (tableau columns). The 8 houses and the 4 shared Foundations in the middle belong to both players.
  • Goal: be the first to empty your Reserve, Hand, and Waste piles completely.
  • Available cards you may play on your turn: the top card of your own Reserve, your currently Drawn card (see below), or the outermost card of any house — yours or your opponent's.
  • An available card can be played to:
    • A Foundation — empty foundations only take an Ace, then build up by suit (A,2,3...K).
    • A House (yours or your opponent's) — one rank lower and the opposite color of that house's top card, or anything if the house is empty.
    • Your opponent's Reserve or Waste pile — "loading" it with a card of the same suit, one rank higher or lower than its current top card. You can keep loading as long as you have a card that fits the new top.
  • Click an available card to select it (highlighted gold), then click a legal destination to play it. Click it again to deselect.
  • Play in any order you like — there's no forced priority here, just make whichever legal move benefits you most.
  • Click Draw to reveal the next Hand card — it becomes available to play just like your Reserve. If you don't (or can't) play it, click your own Waste pile to discard it there, which ends your turn. If you do play it, you may draw again and keep your turn going.
  • If your Hand runs out, clicking Draw instead flips your Waste back into a face-down Hand, without ending your turn.
  • You can never move a card from your own Waste pile, and you can never play onto your own Reserve or Waste (only your opponent's, or via the discard-to-end-turn action above).
  • First player to empty Reserve + Hand + Waste wins!

How to play (Russian Bank / Zank-Patience / Crapette)

  • Each player has their own deck split into a Reserve (top card face up), a Hand (face down), a Waste pile, and 4 Houses (the columns flanking the foundations). The 8 houses and the 8 shared Foundation slots in the middle belong to both players.
  • Goal: be the first to empty your Reserve, Hand, and Waste piles completely.
  • Available cards you may play on your turn: the top card of your own Reserve, your currently Drawn card (see below), or the outermost card of any house — yours or your opponent's.
  • Drag an available card onto:
    • A Foundation slot — an empty slot only takes an Ace, then builds up by suit (A,2,3...K). There are 8 slots since both decks' duplicate ranks need somewhere to go.
    • A House (yours or your opponent's) — one rank lower and the opposite color of that house's top card, or anything if the house is empty.
    • Your opponent's Reserve or Waste pile — "loading" it with a card of the same suit, one rank higher or lower than its current top card. Keep loading as long as you have a card that fits the new top.
  • Drop it back where it started (or anywhere invalid) and the move is simply cancelled.
  • Play in any order you like — there's no forced priority here, just make whichever legal move benefits you most.
  • Tap your Hand pile (or its Draw button) to reveal the next Hand card — it becomes available to play just like your Reserve. If you don't (or can't) play it, drag it onto your own Waste pile to discard it, which ends your turn. If you do play it elsewhere, you may draw again and keep your turn going.
  • If your Hand runs out, tapping it instead flips your Waste back into a face-down Hand, without ending your turn.
  • You can never move a card from your own Waste pile, and you can never play onto your own Reserve or Waste (only your opponent's, or via the discard-to-end-turn drag above).
  • First player to empty Reserve + Hand + Waste wins!
